What exists today

A working space between “I’m fine” and “I need help.”

The repository already contains the practical building blocks of a local-first wellbeing companion. The public app subdomain exposes that packaged experience without publishing the rest of the source tree.

01

Reflect in your own words

Use a journal, stream-of-consciousness capture, or conversational space to slow down and name what is happening.

02

Review patterns over time

Personal dashboards bring notes, assessments, and historical signals together without turning a score into a verdict.

03

Keep control of records

Browser-based storage, import, and export tools are designed to keep the user close to their own information.

04

Choose the model boundary

The application supports locally hosted and configured network model paths. If you choose a network model, prompts and relevant conversation content are sent to that provider under its terms.

Our approach

Technology can create a pause. People create safety.

PreCrisis AI is being built to support earlier awareness and stronger human connection—not to label people, promise prediction, or automate high-stakes decisions.

  1. 1

    Make space

    Capture the situation at the pace and level of detail that feels manageable.

  2. 2

    Find context

    Look across prior notes and assessments for change, repetition, and unanswered needs.

  3. 3

    Stay in charge

    Treat AI output as a prompt for reflection, not a diagnosis, authority, or instruction.

  4. 4

    Choose a human next step

    Reach a trusted person, peer, clinician, crisis counselor, or emergency service when the situation calls for it.

Designed for trust

Clear boundaries are part of the product.

Local-first records

Records stay in browser storage by default. A network model sends prompts and relevant conversation content to its configured provider; review that provider's terms before use.

No invisible emergency promise

The app does not continuously watch a person, call responders, or guarantee that it will recognize a crisis.

Human judgment stays central

Assessments and generated responses support reflection. They do not establish clinical, legal, employment, or public-safety conclusions.

The story

From a veteran mental-health hackathon to a working local-first platform.

PreCrisis AI began with a simple question: can technology help people notice change and involve support before a difficult moment becomes an emergency?

  1. 2023

    Veteran mental-health hackathon

    The initial concept was shaped by a service-connected team and recognized at the Fall In veteran mental-health hackathon.

  2. 2025

    A broader resilience ecosystem

    PreCrisis AI became part of The Wizard Nexus ecosystem, connecting behavioral-health research with secure technology delivery.

  3. Now

    An inspectable product build

    The Arcane repository includes a packaged PreCrisis application with journaling, chat, dashboards, assessments, and user-controlled data tools.
